How each type is treated (Schedule III / AS-21, 23, 27 logic)

  • Parent — your reporting company. Added 100%, line by line.
  • Subsidiary — fully consolidated line by line (100%), then the minority share is carved out as Non-controlling interest. Parent's investment is eliminated and the difference becomes Goodwill / Capital reserve on consolidation.
  • Associate / Joint venture — equity method: only the group's share of profit is added (not line by line), and the carrying value of the investment is uplifted.
  • Branch / Division — clubbed 100% into the parent; no holding %, no minority interest (it is the same legal entity).

How mapping works — you never have to map manually:

  • If you include the "Schedule III head" column, the app uses it directly — this is 100% accurate. You can type the full head name (e.g. Trade receivables), a short code (e.g. debtors, PPE, creditors), or the internal key (e.g. trade_rec). The column may be headed "Schedule III head", "Grouping", "Classification" or "Mapping".
  • If you don't, the app auto-detects the head from the ledger name using a large keyword dictionary (handles Tally-style names, GST/TDS ledgers, sundry debtors/creditors, overdraft, deferred tax, and so on). Each row shows a tag — ● from file, ● auto, or ● unmapped.
  • Anything it can't confidently place is flagged ● unmapped and a banner tells you how many. Unmapped rows are excluded from the statements until they get a head, so nothing is ever silently misclassified. Add them to the head column (or pick from the dropdown) and they're fixed.

Other rules:

  • Put each amount in either Debit or Credit — leave the other blank, no minus signs. Or use a single Amount/Balance column (positive = debit, negative = credit).
  • Previous-year (comparative) figures: add Prev. year Debit and Prev. year Credit columns (also detected as "Prior", "Last year", "PY", or a single "Previous amount"). When present, a Previous-year column flows through the statements, notes and exports automatically. Each year must balance on its own.
  • Header names are flexible (Account / Particulars / Ledger / Name; Debit / Dr; Credit / Cr) and order doesn't matter. Commas in numbers and any "Total / Subtotal" row are ignored automatically.
  • Each entity's trial balance must balance (total debits = total credits) or the consolidated sheet won't tie.

Notes to the Consolidated Financial Statements

Each numbered note supports the corresponding line on the face of the statements (see the Note No. column). Every note reconciles the standalone trial-balance figures to the consolidated amount via the consolidation-adjustment line.

Note 1 — Basis of consolidation and significant accounting policies

This note set covers 4 entities: Sunrise Holdings Ltd (Parent); Bright Ltd (Subsidiary, holding 80%); Sunrise — Pune Branch (Branch); Spark Associates (Associate, holding 30%).

The consolidated financial statements have been prepared in accordance with the presentation requirements of Schedule III to the Companies Act, 2013 and applicable Accounting Standards / Ind AS.

Basis of consolidation: The financial statements of the parent and its subsidiaries have been combined on a line-by-line basis by adding together like items of assets, liabilities, income and expenses. Intra-group balances and intra-group transactions, and the resulting unrealised profits, have been eliminated in full.

Subsidiaries are entities controlled by the parent and are consolidated from the date control is obtained. Goodwill represents the excess of the cost of investment over the parent’s share of net assets at acquisition; a deficit is treated as a capital reserve.

Non-controlling interest (NCI) in the net assets and net results of consolidated subsidiaries is identified and presented separately from the equity attributable to the owners of the parent.

Investments in associates and joint ventures are accounted for using the equity method, under which the group’s share of post-acquisition profits is recognised in the consolidated statement of profit and loss and added to the carrying amount of the investment.

Branches are integral to the reporting entity and have been combined in full (100%).

These statements are prepared from the trial balances submitted for each entity; previous-period (comparative) figures have not been captured in this tool. Goodwill, fair-value adjustments, pre/post-acquisition reserves, deferred tax on consolidation and unrealised-profit eliminations are computed on simplifying assumptions and should be reviewed by a qualified professional.

Important — please read

  • This tool gives a fast, transparent first-pass consolidation. Always have a qualified professional review goodwill, fair-value adjustments, pre/post-acquisition reserves, deferred tax on consolidation, and unrealised-profit eliminations before finalising statutory accounts.
  • Goodwill uses: Cost of investment − Holding % × (Subsidiary share capital + pre-acquisition reserves). Set pre-acquisition reserves per subsidiary in the Entities tab for accurate goodwill.
  • If the balance sheet does not tie, check that every trial balance is balanced (Tab 2) and that each elimination reduces one asset and one liability/equity head (or two offsetting P&L heads).
