How it works
  • Raw → uploader. Every column of the raw Amex export is copied into the uploader shifted one column right (raw A→B … raw M→N), matching the uploader's blue headings. Row count adapts to the raw file; extra last-week rows are cleared.
  • Formula columns. For every data row the tool regenerates the NetSuite formulas (vendor & item internal-ID XLOOKUPs, =C, AF*F, =U, project/customer XLOOKUPs) and the standing defaults (Uploader, credit-card account, US Dollar, New York, entry date, etc.), captured live from your uploader so they always match its current layout. A full recalculation is requested on open.
  • Self-training. Drop past filled uploaders into the training box and the tool records, for every Description, exactly how Item / Vendor / Department were filled (and which department each card-holder tends to use). Training is cumulative, stored in this browser, and applied immediately to anything already loaded.
  • The Description decides — not the merchant name. Predictions read the words in the Description (the notes you add, e.g. "Cold Brew", "Wall LED Light", "Lotion", "Macbook"), so an aggregator like Amazon is booked by what was bought, not collapsed to one item. The tool learns each content word → item from your past files, falls back to a built-in dictionary, and flags the row so you confirm. Priority: a Description seen exactly before replicates (AUTO); otherwise the content words choose the item; only with no distinguishing words does it use the merchant's most-frequent booking (CHOOSE); a brand-new merchant gets a best guess + the card-holder's usual Department (NEW). Card-holder roles (Arielle → Admin, Himanshu → CXO, the marketing cards → Marketing, Umesh → CXO, Steve → Sales) are also learned from training for new people.
  • Filter section. The row under the table headers filters every column — Date, Card holder, Description, Amount, Item, Vendor, Department, Status. Filters combine (AND) and stack with the tabs; the Amount box understands >100, <=50, =20. "Clear filters" resets them. Use it to see, say, every Travel item, every Marketing row, or everything on one card.
  • Raw → uploader & formulas. The raw Amex export is copied into the uploader's blue headings (raw A→B … M→N); row count adapts. For every row the NetSuite formulas (vendor & item internal-ID XLOOKUPs, =C, AF*F, =U, project/customer XLOOKUPs) and standing defaults (Uploader, credit-card account, US Dollar, New York, entry date) are rebuilt from your uploader and recalculated on open.
  • Safety. Confirmed rows are also remembered; export/import memory to move between machines. Output is your uploader with only the touched cells rewritten — all other sheets, styles and dropdown validations are preserved byte-for-byte.
