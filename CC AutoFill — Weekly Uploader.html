How it works
  • Raw → uploader. Every column of the raw Amex export is copied into the uploader shifted one column right (raw A→B … raw M→N), matching the uploader's blue headings. Row count adapts to the raw file; extra last-week rows are cleared.
  • Formula columns. For every data row the tool regenerates the NetSuite formulas (vendor & item internal-ID XLOOKUPs, =C, AF*F, =U, project/customer XLOOKUPs) and the standing defaults (Uploader, credit-card account, US Dollar, New York, entry date, etc.), captured live from your uploader so they always match its current layout. A full recalculation is requested on open.
  • Department. Defaults to the card-holder's role (Arielle → Admin, Himanshu → CXO, the marketing cards → Marketing, Umesh → CXO, Steve → Operations : COE : Managed Services). If that exact merchant was booked to a different department before, the row is flagged CONFLICT so you confirm.
  • Item & Vendor. From merchant history first; new merchants get a flagged best guess (vendor-name match, item keywords). Merchants booked inconsistently before (Amazon, Uber Eats, AmexGBT…) are never guessed — you pick from their history.
  • Learning & safety. Confirmed rows are remembered in this browser; export/import to move between machines. Output is your uploader with only the touched cells rewritten — all other sheets, styles and dropdown validations are preserved.
